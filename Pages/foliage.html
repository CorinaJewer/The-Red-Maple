Where to See Fall Foliage in Canada


Foliage is a fancy term refering to the leaves on a tree. Fall Foliage refers to the changing and falling of the leaves on a tree. Here in Canada, the leaves begin to change around mid September with the main performance occuring around the second to third week of October.

Every fall, the leaves of a Red Maple undergo a transformation, turning red, orange and yellow. This transformation results from the breakdown of chlorophyll in the leaves as the tree prepares for the winter months. People often visit parks and natural areas known for their Red Maple populations to enjoy the stunning fall foliage displays.


There are many wonderful places across Canada to view these vibrant trees at their peak. Check out our list for you to consider on your next fall adventure. You will not be disappointed!

  1. Algonquin Provincial Park, Ontario
  2. Niagara Falls, Ontario
  3. Hautes-Gordes-de-la-Riviere-Malbaie National Park, Quebec
  4. Montreal, Quebec
  5. Halifax, Nova Scotia
  6. Mont Tremblant, Quebec
  7. Vancouver, British Columbia
  8. Canadian Rocky Mountains, Alberta
  9. Cape Breton, Nova Scotia
  10. Corner Brook, Newfoundland and Labrador
