Distribution


The Red Maple is one of the most abundant and widespread trees in eastern North America. This can be attributed to the fact that it's a generalist species which is one that can tolerate a wide range of habitat conditions and uses many different types of resources. They range from the eastern Canadian cities of Corner Brook, Newfoundland to the atlantic province of Nova Scotia. Red Maples can been seen throughout Quebec and Ontario, continuing west to southern Manitoba. The range extends southward through the eastern United States, reaching states like Florida and East Texas.

Habitat


The Acer rumbrum is oftem found in swamps and moist soils although it can can grow quite fine in drier soils. Adaptable roots help this species grow in varying soil types. If the tree is planted in wet soil, it grows a short taproot and extensive lateral roots to soak up water at the surface. When red maples are planted in dry areas, the exact opposite occurs to compensate. Deep, moist, acidic soil will result in the healthiest red maples. Moderately shade-tolerant, Red Maple will grow almost anywhere.
