Uses of the Red Maple Tree

Landscaping

Red Maples are widely planted in both backyards and commercial landscapes. Their popularity is largely due to their brillant fall colors, adaptability to various soil conditions and shade offered on a sunny day.

Food for Animals

The maple tree provides food for various wildlife species including birds, mammals and insects. Squirrels for example tap the trees, nibble on the end of branches and lick up sap. Larger mammals such as deer and elk consume the leaves.

Syrup

The sap of the Red Maple tree can be used to make syrup, although the Sugar Maple tree is the preferred choice of commercial producers. The syrup can also be furthered processed into maple candy, fudge and sugar.

Woodworking

Red Maple wood is considered a softer wood. It can be used for making such things as musical instruments, kitchen cabinets and furniture. Since much of the wood is deformed, only select pieces can be used for making furniture. When it comes to firewood, don't make it your first pick, the quality is only medium.
